Seoul - the capital of South Korea

Seoul, city and capital of South Korea (the Republic of Korea). It is located on the Han River (Han-gang) in the northwestern part of the country, with the city center some 37 miles (60 km) inland from the Yellow Sea (west). Seoul is the cultural, economic, and political center of South Korea.

At the heart of Seoul, you can find historic landmarks, such as Gyeongbokgung Palace, that coexist alongside modern shopping hubs like Myeongdong. Rich in cultural and artistic assets, this area also serves as South Korea's economic and financial center, bustling with business activities. The concentration of major tourist attractions makes it a perennial destination for visitors.

The area is alive with youth culture and innovation, with Hongdae Street buzzing with young people's energy and creativity. This area is a hub for street performances, art exhibitions, and diverse cultural events, fostering a continuous exchange of cultural inspiration. Here, nature and the urban environment blend seamlessly, providing numerous relaxing spaces, like Ansan Jarak-gil and more.

As the center of education and academia, it houses several universities. Alongside vibrant local markets like Cheongnyangni Market, this area is bustling with economic activity. Natural retreats, like Bukhansan Mountain, are nearby, allowing residents and visitors to enjoy nature within the city. The area also features various cultural spaces that offer a unique mix of vintage and modernity.

These districts serve as business and financial hubs, with Yeongdeungpo and Yeouido at its heart. It is densely populated with corporate offices in high-rise buildings and offers the convenience of numerous large shopping malls. Additionally, beautiful natural spaces, like one of the many Hangang Park locations, provide opportunities for leisurely relaxation amid the bustling city.

You'll notice scenic natural landscape connected to the Hangang River, seamlessly integrated with various cultural facilities. The areas around Samseong-dong and Gangnam Station are major shopping and entertainment hubs, maintaining a vibrant atmosphere even at night. Well-equipped sports and leisure facilities and parks, such as the Jamsil Sports Complex and Olympic Park, offer a perfect spot for enjoying a wide range of recreational activities.
